About IFRS 16

IFRS 16 is the International Financial Reporting Standard that sets out the principles for recognition, measurement, presentation and disclosure of leases. The standard was published by the International Accounting Standards Board (IASB) in January 2016 and became effective on January 1, 2019.

Under IFRS 16, lessees are required to recognize assets and liabilities for all leases with a term of more than 12 months, unless the underlying asset is of low value. This involves:

  • Recognizing a right-of-use asset representing its right to use the underlying leased asset
  • Recognizing a lease liability representing its obligation to make lease payments
  • Recognizing depreciation of right-of-use assets and interest on lease liabilities in the income statement
